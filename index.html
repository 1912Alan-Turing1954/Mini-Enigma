Enigma Machine





This is a Enigma Machine containing only 1 rotor offset, than the normal 5 or 3 rotors.


The "Enigma" was made in 1918 at the end of World War 1 by Arthur Scherbius,


a German electrical engineer who lived from 1878 to 1929.


Enigma is a famous encryption machine used by the Germans during WWII to transmit coded messages.


It was deemed uncrackable, do to it's 158,962,555,217,826,360,000 possible seetings/encryptions.
